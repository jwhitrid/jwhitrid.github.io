Ethics for Software Engineers

The IEEE created a code of ethics for software engineers to abide to. There are Eight principles that they should follow when conducting business:

  1. Public: Act consistently with the public interest.
  2. Client and Employer: Act in the best interest of their client and employer.
  3. Product: Ensure that their product meets the highest of standards.
  4. Judgement: Maintain integrity and independence in professional judgment.
  5. Management: Managers should promote an ethical approach to software development.
  6. Profession: Advance the integrity of the profession consistent with public interest.
  7. Colleagues: Fair and support fellow colleagues.
  8. Self: Continue learning in their profession and promote and ethical approach to their practice.

Ethics for Data Scientists

Data Scientists shall follow a code of ethics as well. They shall be equipped with avoiding harm and building trust with public interest. There are some specific considerations for them to adhere to, professionally:

  1. Protecting Data Privacy
  2. Ensuring Data Justice and Fairness
  3. Promoting Transparency
  4. Controlling Confidential Data
  5. Ensuring the Accuracy of Data
  6. Remaining Accountable
